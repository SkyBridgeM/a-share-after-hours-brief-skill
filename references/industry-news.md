# Industry and Supply-Chain News

Use this reference when industry, upstream, downstream, peer, or policy news may explain the specified stocks' after-hours review.

## Source priority

1. Company announcements and exchange disclosures.
2. Official regulators, ministries, exchanges, and industry associations.
3. Wind financial news.
4. Major financial media and credible industry publications.

## Rules

- Prefer recent news from the report date or immediately preceding trading sessions.
- Include source links for material external references.
- Mark rumor-like information as unverified and do not build a strong conclusion on it.
- Do not use web news as a substitute for Wind price, K-line, announcement, or financial data.
- Explain relevance to the specified stock's value chain; do not turn the section into a general market-news digest.
- Industry or supply-chain news can support the information/news evidence for `向上` / `维持震荡` / `向下`, but should not be the sole basis for a strong directional judgment without price-volume confirmation.

## Value-chain lens

Before selecting industry news, map the company into a compact value-chain lens:

1. **Upstream**: raw materials, components, energy, equipment, supply constraints, input prices, and upstream capacity changes.
2. **Own segment**: the company's main product/service, pricing power, production/sales rhythm, utilization, inventory, and competitive position.
3. **Downstream**: end demand, customers, channels, order cycles, subsidies, export/import demand, and application scenarios.
4. **Peers/substitutes**: peer announcements, price cuts, capacity expansion, technology substitution, and sector leader feedback.
5. **Policy/macro bridge**: policies, regulation, trade frictions, interest rates, exchange rates, or macro data only when they clearly affect one of the links above.

Report only the links with material current signals. If no material upstream/downstream signal is found, say `上下游暂无明显新增信号` rather than padding with generic news.

## Relevance test

Use a news item only if at least one of these questions has a clear answer:

- Does it affect the company's cost, supply availability, or gross margin pressure?
- Does it affect downstream demand, order visibility, customer capex, or channel inventory?
- Does it change the sector's pricing, competition, policy constraint, or technology path?
- Does it explain today's relative strength/weakness versus market or sector?
- Does it create a concrete confirmation or invalidation variable for the next session?

When relevance is indirect, label it as indirect and keep the conclusion cautious.

## Suggested output shape

For each stock or shared sector, use short bullets:

- `上游`: cost/supply signal and likely impact.
- `公司所处环节`: product/pricing/competition signal and likely impact.
- `下游`: demand/order/customer/channel signal and likely impact.
- `同业/替代`: peer or substitute signal and likely impact.
- `结论`: whether the news side is supportive, neutral/mixed, or pressuring for the next-session tendency.
